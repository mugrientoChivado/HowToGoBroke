Method #1: Spend Years on a Career that Won't Get You a Stable Job

It's always boring to go for the safe options. You could study logistics, law, engineering, architecture, but those are hard and boring. And we don't like hard and boring! We like friendly and fun and quirky, and most importantly, prestigious-sounding! That way, it sounds like we're wasting spending our time on something grandious and productive! So our best bets are along the lines of: musical composition, any art career whatsoever, animation, audio and/or video editing, and of course, programming. Sure, everyone says that programming is the future, but can a programmer fix a broken pipeline, pave the streets, or sell you a cold refreshing soda at 2 am? I don't think so. They also say that music is the universal language, but can a musician mediate between nations for world peace, or clean up the streets or even just patch up a punctured tube in your bike? I sure as hell don't think so.

This is one of the most typical methods to go broke. Get up on your saddle of self-importance and waste your life telling yourself you're doing something useful for society and for yourself!
